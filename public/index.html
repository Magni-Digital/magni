How Magni works

Every morning the tool hands you a fresh set of small practices with a genuinely weak website — each with one true thing wrong and a first line drafted. You confirm it, put it in your words, and send it yourself. The tool finds and checks; you write and send.

Where leads come from

  • Already loaded: a few hundred qualified practices are here now — a couple weeks of work.
  • Adding more: paste new finds into the Magni import Google Sheet (one row each — at minimum a company + website). That sheet is your inbox; overnight the tool checks them and adds the good ones. Use Sheet headers for the columns.

What it does to each practice (overnight)

  • Opens their site and runs objective checks — no HTTPS, not mobile-friendly, slow, stale copyright, thin page, no booking, dead links.
  • Keeps only the ones with a real, checked weakness (clean sites are dropped).
  • Drafts one true opening line — it can only say what it actually verified, so it never makes something up.
  • Grabs the contact email off the site and flags whether it looks valid.

Your two tabs

  • Today's queue — qualified practices with findings + a drafted line. Your daily work.
  • Research · no site — no website found. Open their LinkedIn; if you find a site, paste it on the card and it gets checked.

Working a card

  1. Read the checked findings — the true things wrong with their site.
  2. Read the draft line, open their site to confirm it's true, and rewrite it in your voice. The draft is a running start; your rewrite is the work.
  3. Copy it (and the email), paste into your own email, and send it yourself.
  4. Hit Mark sent (or Skip).
  5. At day's end, Download CSV → paste into the Magni CRM sheet. That sheet is your record.

Worth knowing

  • You won't lose leads — sent ones retire, skipped come back in 30 days, untouched ones stay.
  • Notes live on each card (and ride along in your CSV). More info shows size, industry, revenue, LinkedIn.
  • It refreshes itself every morning — just open the page.

The one rule: the tool finds the practice, checks the site, and drafts the line — you always confirm and send. It never reaches out to anyone on its own.
